[*] on June 6, FC Tirol were refused a professional license for the
    2002/03 season (in an earlier decision, they had obtained it but
    were to start with a 5 point penalty) due to grave financial
    problems; due to this, Admira/Wacker Mödling will remain at the
    first level.
    Sturm will take Tirol's place in the final qualifying round for
    the Champions' League, GAK obtain Sturm's place in the second
    qualifying round for the Champions' League, Austria Wien obtain
    GAK's place in the first round of the UEFA Cup and FC Kärnten
    obtain Austria's place in the qualifying round for the UEFA Cup.
    FC Tirol are refounded as FC Wacker Innsbruck (the traditional
    name) and will form a 'Spielgemeinschaft' (playing community)
    with WSG Wattens of the 3rd level (Regionalliga West), and will
    consequently play as WSG Swarovski Wattens/Wacker Tirol in 2002/03.
